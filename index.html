How it started

We burned a lot of garlic getting here

The first batch was terrible. Way too much salt, not enough crunch. But we kept going. Different chilis, different oil temps, different roast times. Somewhere around batch twenty, people started asking for extra jars.

We still make it the same way: in our kitchen, one batch at a time. No factory, no production line. Just a stove, a mortar, and a lot of patience.

The Making

How we make it

1

Pick

We're picky about our chilis. If they're not right, they don't go in.

2

Roast

Low and slow over open flame. The kitchen smells incredible for days.

3

Crush

Mortar and pestle. Takes forever but you can taste the difference.

4

Jar

Jarred warm so it stays crunchy. Best within the first few weeks.
